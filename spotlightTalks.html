General Instructions

Spotlight Talk

Slides:
• Fonts should be large enough.
• Don’t use light colors like yellow or green, they are usually not that visible.
• Use figures and/or animations to visualize the ideas.
• Everything that is on the slides should be explained.
• Have your slides ready at least 2 days before your presentation and upload them on Canvas.

Presentation (5-7 minutes in total):
• Spend some time on introducing the problem to the audience and motivate why this research is important (1-2 slides, 1-2 minutes).
• Summarise the main aspects of the paper (2-3 slides, 3-4 minutes).
• Use 1 slide to state what you liked (or didn’t like) about the paper (1 minute).
• Practice the presentation at least twice in front of other students before the spotlight talk and incorporate feedback.

Essay
• Each essay has to be 5-7 pages (excluding references) in Springer LNCS style using Latex.
• Motivate the topic and review relevant literature (1-2 pages).
• Summarize the novelty of the paper and compare it to previous approaches (2 pages).
• What future work would you suggest to do based on the results presented in the paper? (1 page).
• Do a critical review of the paper. What is interesting about the paper? Do you have some criticism? (1-2 pages)
• Upload your essay as a zip file (including all sources) on Canvas by the deadline.

Marking scheme for the seminar

• slides: 0..6 marks (readability, quality of figures, typos, ...)
• presentation: 0..6 marks (free speech, body language, time limit, ...)
• knowledge: 0..6 marks (details missing, like/dislike of article, ...)
• response to questions: 0..2 marks
• --> In total you can get 20 marks. As an assignment feedback, you might have a sequence like “5,5,5,1” and some text by us --> This means that the talk was actually pretty good (80%), and there were just minor issues with the slides, the presentation stile, and maybe you did not explain everything extremely well (including the answers to our questions).

Marking scheme for the essay

• document: 0..6 marks (format, quality of figures, typos, ...)
• content: 0..14 marks (introduction to the content, summary of the novelty, critical review, are you omitting details, ...)
• --> In total you can get 20 marks. As an assignment feedback, you might have a sequence like "4,12" and some text by us --> This means that the essay was actually pretty good (80%), and there were just minor issues.
